About

I am a Staff Research Scientist at CFM (Capital Fund Management) in Paris, where I co-lead the CFM AI Lab with Eric Vanden-Eijnden. Our group develops foundational methods in generative modeling and statistical inference, with applications to quantitative science and finance. I supervise a team of research interns and two PhD students.

My work is conducted in close collaboration with Stéphane Mallat (ENS / Flatiron Institute) and Jean-Philippe Bouchaud (CFM), among others.

I hold a PhD in statistics (2023), supervised by Nicolas Dobigeon and Pierre Chainais. During my doctorate, I was a visiting scholar at the Department of Statistics, University of Oxford, working with Arnaud Doucet.

Research Interests

Stochastic Interpolants & Flow Matching

Building generative models and samplers through continuous-time interpolation between distributions, with connections to score-based diffusion and optimal transport.

Deep Generative Models

Normalizing flows and diffusion-based models for density estimation, sampling, and likelihood-free inference.

Bayesian Inference

Plug-and-play methods that embed deep generative priors into posterior sampling, enabling scalable inference in high-dimensional inverse problems.

Optimal Transport

Computational and geometric tools for comparing probability distributions, including sliced-Wasserstein distances and flow-based OT maps.
